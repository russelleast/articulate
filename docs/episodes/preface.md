# Preface

## Welcome to the Articulate Journal

Welcome.

This repository is not a product blog, nor is it a retrospective describing a completed project. It is a public engineering journal documenting the design, implementation and evolution of **Articulate**—an experiment in AI-native architecture.

The software itself is only part of the story.

The real purpose of this repository is to explore architectural thinking in public. Every significant idea, architectural decision, experiment, success and failure will be recorded as the project evolves. Rather than presenting a polished solution at the end, this journal captures the reasoning that leads to that solution.

I have always believed that understanding *why* a decision was made is often more valuable than the decision itself.

## Why Build in Public?

Software architecture is full of uncertainty.

Architects rarely begin with complete information. We work with assumptions, incomplete evidence, competing priorities and changing requirements. Many of the most important architectural decisions are made long before any code is written, yet those conversations and thought processes are rarely documented.

Instead, we typically see the finished diagrams, the Architecture Decision Records and the implementation. The questions, trade-offs and discarded ideas are often lost.

I want this journal to be different.

Building in public creates an opportunity to record not just the destination, but the journey. Some ideas will prove successful. Others will be abandoned. Many will evolve into something completely different from their original form. That is not failure—it is simply how architecture works.

## Why Now?

The software industry is experiencing one of the fastest periods of change in its history.

Large language models, coding agents, context engineering, agentic systems and AI-assisted development are changing how software is designed and built. Many of the assumptions that shaped software architecture over the past decade are being challenged.

Rather than treating AI as another tool in the toolbox, I believe it has the potential to become a genuine architectural collaborator—helping architects discover information, challenge assumptions, reason about trade-offs and preserve organisational knowledge.

Whether that vision proves correct remains to be seen.

Articulate exists to explore that question.

## What Is Articulate?

Articulate is not intended to become another documentation repository or diagramming tool.

Instead, it is an exploration into **architectural intelligence**.

The long-term vision is to investigate whether AI can help architects better understand complex systems, maintain architectural knowledge over time, reason about change and improve the quality of architectural decision-making.

Throughout this journal I will explore ideas such as:

* Architecture as a continuously evolving body of knowledge.
* Conversation as the primary interface for architectural exploration.
* Evidence and confidence as first-class architectural concepts.
* Versioned architecture baselines.
* AI-assisted discovery and reasoning.
* Architectural impact analysis.
* Capability-driven architecture.
* Human and AI collaboration throughout the software lifecycle.

These ideas are intentionally ambitious. Some may prove impractical, while others may become foundational. The only way to discover which is which is to build, experiment and learn.

## What to Expect

This journal is organised into a series of episodes, each focusing on a specific topic or architectural question.

Not every episode will contain code.

Some will explore architectural principles or design decisions. Others will document experiments, prototypes or implementation details. Where appropriate, episodes will conclude with an outcome, whether that is an architectural decision, a prototype, a lesson learned or simply a new question worth investigating.

The intention is that every episode moves the project forward in some meaningful way.

## Scope

This journal records:

* Architectural principles.
* Requirements and constraints.
* Research and experiments.
* Architectural decisions.
* System design.
* Implementation.
* Operational considerations.
* AI-native architecture.
* Lessons learned.
* Future ideas and open questions.

It deliberately documents both successful and unsuccessful experiments, because understanding why an approach did not work is often just as valuable as understanding why another one did.

## A Personal Note

Articulate began as a way to scratch my own itch.

Over many years working as a software architect, I repeatedly encountered the same frustrations: fragmented knowledge, outdated documentation, uncertain assumptions and the constant effort required simply to understand the current state of a system before meaningful change could begin.

Like many of my personal projects, Articulate started as an excuse to learn something new. Over time it evolved into something much more ambitious—a long-term exploration of what software architecture might become in the age of AI.

I do not claim to have all the answers.

In fact, many of the questions that drive this project remain unanswered.

That is precisely why this journal exists.

## An Invitation

If these topics interest you, I hope you will follow along.

Whether you are an architect, engineer, researcher or simply curious about the future of AI-native systems, I hope the journal provides useful ideas, practical experiments and, perhaps most importantly, insight into the architectural thinking behind them.

Welcome to the journey.
