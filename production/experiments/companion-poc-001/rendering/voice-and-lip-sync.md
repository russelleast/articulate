# Voice And Lip-Sync

## Voice Position

Russell remains the narrator. The companion is a visual presenter and guide, not a voice clone or autonomous author.

## POC Voice Options

- Temporary scratch narration by Russell for timing and review.
- Human-recorded final narration only after script approval.
- Synthetic placeholder voice only if clearly labelled as timing audio and never used for publication.

## Lip-Sync Position

Lip-sync is optional for this proof. The experiment should not fail merely because a companion mouth does not move. A credible non-deceptive presenter can use limited animation, gestures, gaze and scene transitions.

## Disclosure

The proof should include a short spoken or visible disclosure:

> The voice and argument are Russell's; the companion is an AI-generated visual presenter.

This wording may be refined in review, but the meaning should remain plain.

## Review Questions

- Does lip-sync make the companion feel like it is impersonating the author?
- Does no lip-sync feel acceptable for a technical essay?
- Does the companion's presence support pacing or distract from the source?
- Is the disclosure sufficient without overwhelming the opening?
