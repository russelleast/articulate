# Rendering Options

## Option 1 - Still Companion With Recorded Voice

Use companion stills or lightly animated loops while Russell's recorded voice carries the narration.

Strengths:

- Low impersonation risk.
- Avoids poor lip-sync.
- Fastest path to test source-to-storyboard coherence.

Risks:

- May not sufficiently test companion motion.
- Presenter scenes may feel static.

## Option 2 - Stylised Avatar With Limited Lip-Sync

Use a stylised companion renderer with short lip-synced opening and closing scenes.

Strengths:

- Tests the intended companion-led entry and return.
- Reveals vendor constraints early.

Risks:

- Lip-sync may dominate review.
- Vendor artefacts may influence design decisions too soon.

## Option 3 - Illustrated Presenter With Motion Layers

Use a 2D illustrated companion with minimal mouth movement, parallax and gesture.

Strengths:

- Strong non-deceptive visual stance.
- Keeps focus on narration and diagrams.

Risks:

- May feel less like a reusable presenter.
- Requires coherent illustration and animation workflow.

## Recommended POC Path

Start with Option 1 or Option 3. Use Option 2 only for a small comparison test, not as a commitment. The first evidence needed is whether the visual narrative works, not whether a vendor can produce a perfect presenter.
