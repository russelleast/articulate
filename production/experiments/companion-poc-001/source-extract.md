## Selected Source Sections

### Introduction

Selected claims:

- Modern technology is capable, but understanding the architecture itself remains hard.
- Architects spend time answering questions about the current architecture, its rationale, changes, decisions and whether documentation can be trusted.

Short source excerpt:

> "Yet despite all of these advances, one problem remains remarkably consistent. Understanding the architecture itself."

### The Reality of Modern Architecture

Selected claims:

- Architectural knowledge is scattered across source code, Infrastructure as Code, ADRs, diagrams, slide decks, monitoring platforms and people's memories.
- Each source contains part of the truth, but none contains the whole picture.
- Architecture is a living system rather than a static asset.

Short source excerpt:

> "Each source contains part of the truth. None contains the whole picture."

### The Vision

Selected claims:

- Articulate began as a side project and grew into a broader question.
- The central question is whether architecture can become a continuously evolving body of knowledge rather than disconnected documents.
- The project explores AI as a collaborator, not as a replacement for architects.

Short source excerpt:

> "Can architecture become a continuously evolving body of knowledge instead of a collection of disconnected documents?"

## Why This Extract Fits The Prototype

This extract contains a complete short-form arc:

- the persistent problem: understanding architecture;
- the cause: fragmented, changing and incomplete knowledge;
- the project shift: Articulate as an exploration into a continuously evolving architectural knowledge base.

It also supports the required visual movement: companion introduction, fragmented source imagery, concept diagram and companion close.

## Material Intentionally Excluded

- "Architecture Is About Change" is excluded from the spoken slice to keep the prototype under 90 seconds. Its idea remains implicit in the visual movement from current fragments to a future knowledge model.
- "The Limits of Today's Tools" is excluded to avoid compressing a nuanced tool argument into a throwaway line.
- "Why Now?" is excluded from narration, except for the careful implication that AI is part of the experiment. This avoids over-claiming AI capability in a short proof.
- "Principles Before Features", "Success" and "Looking Ahead" are excluded because they belong to the full episode treatment.

- Whether "AI-assisted" should appear in the spoken disclosure or only visually. The current draft includes a brief spoken disclosure and avoids labouring the point.
- Whether the central question should be shown exactly as written or shortened for readability. The current storyboard uses the exact question as the final concept beat.
