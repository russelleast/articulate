# Direction B - Architectural Companion

## Intent

A designed visual guide that sits between presenter, architectural assistant and research companion. This is the default candidate for the proof of concept because it emphasises guidance rather than performance.

## Visual Characteristics

- Clearly artificial, semi-human presence with calm, readable facial cues.
- Softer architectural styling than Direction A: warm neutral clothing, clean lines, no sci-fi costume.
- Subtle diagrammatic motifs in the environment rather than on the body.
- Distinct enough to recur across episodes without becoming a mascot.
- Compatible with journal pages, repository views and abstract diagrams.

## Motion

- Presenter-like stillness with occasional deliberate gestures.
- Smooth attention shifts from viewer to source material.
- Transition gestures that allow the scene to move away from the companion.

## Strengths

- Supports the companion specification's guide role.
- Less likely to imply an autonomous expert or replacement architect.
- Flexible across opening, transition and closing scenes.

## Risks

- May become too neutral if not given a recognisable silhouette.
- Needs careful distinction from generic training-video presenters.
- Requires a repeatable visual recipe if carried into future episodes.

## POC Use

Use for the first still-frame and motion exploration. Evaluate whether it can introduce and close the slice while leaving the central visual explanation to diagrams and source imagery.
