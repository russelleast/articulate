# Direction C - Illustrated Presenter

## Intent

An illustrated, editorial presenter with a hand-drawn or painterly technical-documentary style. This direction tests whether a less avatar-like companion avoids deception while still giving the video a coherent guide.

## Visual Characteristics

- 2D or lightly animated illustrated figure.
- Ink, pencil, or soft editorial rendering rather than 3D avatar realism.
- Architectural sketchbook atmosphere.
- Expressive but restrained face and posture.
- Can blend naturally into diagrams and whiteboard sequences.

## Motion

- Limited animation: blink, small posture shift, gesture, parallax.
- Scene transitions can be drawn or sketched rather than lip-synced.
- Mouth movement can be simplified if voice remains Russell's.

## Strengths

- Low risk of impersonation.
- Strong fit for architectural essay tone.
- May reduce dependency on lip-sync quality.

## Risks

- Could feel less present as a presenter.
- Requires a coherent illustration style across assets.
- May make the production feel more handcrafted and less like a reusable AI-native media system.

## POC Use

Use as a fallback if lip-sync and avatar realism become distracting constraints. It may be particularly useful for a no-lip-sync proof with voiceover and companion presence.
