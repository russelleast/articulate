# Direction A - Digital Architect

## Intent

A recognisably human-shaped but clearly stylised technical presenter. This direction tests whether the companion can feel credible to architects without becoming photorealistic or deceptive.

## Visual Characteristics

- Human-shaped silhouette with simplified facial detail.
- Clean architectural clothing cues: structured jacket, neutral layers, no corporate uniform.
- Subtle digital surface treatment: soft linework, restrained material highlights, faint construction-grid accents.
- Calm expression range and minimal gestural vocabulary.
- Works in studio, repository and diagram-adjacent scenes.

## Motion

- Small head turns and eye-line shifts toward diagrams or source paths.
- Hand gestures limited to framing, indicating and transitioning.
- No exaggerated performance or comic timing.

## Strengths

- Familiar enough for presenter scenes.
- Clearly positioned as a designed AI visual presence.
- Strong fit for opening and closing shots.

## Risks

- Can drift towards generic corporate avatar if rendered too realistically.
- Can feel too cold if the digital treatment dominates.
- Requires careful review to avoid any resemblance to Russell.

## POC Use

Use as a comparison direction for companion stills and short motion tests. Do not treat it as the default unless it outperforms Direction B on trust and restraint.
