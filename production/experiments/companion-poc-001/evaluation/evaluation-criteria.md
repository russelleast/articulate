# Evaluation Criteria

## Source Fidelity

- Every material claim traces to Episode 0001 or a labelled production document.
- No fabricated dates, motivations, sequence of events or technical conclusions.
- AI capability is framed as exploratory and augmentative.

## Editorial Coherence

- The 60-90 second slice has a clear opening, middle and close.
- The visual sequence makes the architectural problem easier to understand.
- The companion introduces and concludes without dominating the argument.

## Companion Integrity

- The companion is clearly artificial.
- The companion does not resemble Russell.
- The companion is not presented as author, narrator or autonomous expert.
- Disclosure is plain and proportionate.

## Visual Quality

- Includes at least one non-slide visual sequence.
- Includes one concept diagram or visual transformation.
- On-screen text is sparse, readable and source-grounded.
- The visual tone feels like an architectural essay, not corporate training.

## Production Learning

- The experiment exposes rendering, lip-sync, asset, assembly and review constraints.
- The artefacts are sufficient for a future rough cut.
- Findings can inform later ADRs or production workflow changes.
