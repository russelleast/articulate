# Motion Language

Motion in Articulate media should aid comprehension. It should help the viewer understand sequence, relationship, emphasis and transition.

## Principles

- Motion should have an editorial purpose.
- Transitions should be restrained and consistent.
- Diagrams may build progressively when the argument benefits from it.
- Camera movement should be slow enough for reading and reflection.
- Motion should never compensate for unclear structure.

## Appropriate Uses

- revealing a diagram step by step;
- moving from journal text to a visual interpretation;
- transitioning between reflective and explanatory sections;
- highlighting a repository path, decision, or architectural relationship;
- introducing and dismissing companion presence cleanly.

## Inappropriate Uses

- constant movement behind important text;
- fast decorative transitions;
- motion that implies certainty where the episode is exploratory;
- animated effects that make diagrams harder to read;
- avatar gestures that distract from the argument.

## Accessibility

Motion should respect viewers who need slower pacing or who rely on captions. Important meaning should not depend on a quick animation alone. Subtitles, transcripts and narration should carry the argument even when motion is not visible.
