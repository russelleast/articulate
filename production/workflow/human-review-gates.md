# Human Review Gates

Human review protects the integrity of the canonical episode as it moves into generated and assembled media.

## 1. Source Episode Approved

Checks: the written episode is ready to serve as canonical source.

Can be automated: metadata presence, source path checks, link checks.

Cannot be automated: whether the argument is ready for publication.

## 2. Production Plan Approved

Checks: the proposed treatment preserves the episode's meaning and uses appropriate media.

Can be automated: template completeness, missing fields, source reference presence.

Cannot be automated: editorial judgement about narrative shape, tone and emphasis.

## 3. Narration Approved

Checks: the spoken script is accurate, clear and traceable.

Can be automated: source reference coverage, unsupported claim detection, segment formatting.

Cannot be automated: whether the narration sounds like Russell and carries the intended emphasis.

## 4. Storyboard Approved

Checks: the visual plan supports the episode without adding unsupported claims.

Can be automated: scene numbering, required fields, source reference presence.

Cannot be automated: whether the visual pacing and interpretation are editorially right.

## 5. Companion Scenes Approved

Checks: companion usage is appropriate, disclosed and not deceptive.

Can be automated: presence of disclosure notes and companion mode fields.

Cannot be automated: whether the visual presence feels credible, restrained and non-misleading.

## 6. Diagrams and Slides Technically Reviewed

Checks: technical accuracy, readability and source alignment.

Can be automated: broken links, syntax checks, contrast heuristics, text length warnings.

Cannot be automated: whether the diagram faithfully represents the architectural idea.

## 7. Rough Cut Reviewed

Checks: assembled video communicates clearly and respects the approved plan.

Can be automated: duration, missing assets, caption sync checks.

Cannot be automated: pacing, coherence, editorial quality and viewer experience.

## 8. Accessibility Reviewed

Checks: captions, transcript, readable visuals, audio clarity and non-visual access to important meaning.

Can be automated: caption file presence, transcript presence, basic contrast checks.

Cannot be automated: whether accessibility support is genuinely useful.

## 9. Publication Metadata Reviewed

Checks: title, description, chapters, links, disclosure, thumbnail and platform metadata.

Can be automated: required field checks, link validation, disclosure presence.

Cannot be automated: whether the metadata frames the episode honestly and clearly.

## 10. Final Publication Approved

Checks: all required gates are complete and the final package is suitable for release.

Can be automated: checklist completion and artefact presence.

Cannot be automated: final accountability for publication.
