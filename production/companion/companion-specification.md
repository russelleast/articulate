# Companion Specification

## Purpose

The Articulate Companion is a visual presenter for derived media. It supports explanation, pacing and continuity while keeping authorship with Russell and the canonical written episode.

## Role

The companion acts as a guide through the episode's visual representation. It introduces, connects and concludes. It does not invent claims, replace human authorship or act as a mascot.

## Relationship to Russell

Russell provides:

- authorship;
- architectural judgement;
- recorded narration;
- editorial approval;
- final accountability.

The companion provides:

- designed visual presence;
- presenter-like continuity;
- scene transitions;
- attention direction;
- visual framing around diagrams and demonstrations.

## Characteristics

The companion should be:

- calm;
- thoughtful;
- credible;
- technically literate;
- restrained;
- curious;
- visually distinctive;
- clearly artificial rather than photorealistically deceptive.

The companion should not be:

- comic relief;
- a mascot;
- overly futuristic;
- cyberpunk;
- a generic corporate presenter;
- excessively expressive;
- presented as an autonomous author;
- portrayed as replacing the human architect.

## Frequency of Appearance

Use the companion sparingly. For most episodes, it should appear at the opening, closing and selected transitions. It should not remain on screen throughout diagrams, code demonstrations or screen recordings unless its presence adds clear editorial value.

## Scene Guidance

Appropriate:

- episode introduction;
- central question framing;
- transition from problem to principle;
- transition from explanation to repository evidence;
- closing reflection.

Inappropriate:

- dense technical diagram walkthroughs;
- code demonstrations requiring full attention;
- scenes where a source excerpt should be read directly;
- moments requiring emotional authenticity from the human author.

## Disclosure

Every publication channel should disclose that the companion is an AI-created visual presenter. The disclosure should be plain and non-defensive.

## Open Design Decisions

- Companion name.
- Gender presentation.
- Exact appearance.
- Rendering style.
- Vendor or production tool.
- Whether the companion has recurring visual props or environment.
- How much motion is appropriate for long-form episodes.
