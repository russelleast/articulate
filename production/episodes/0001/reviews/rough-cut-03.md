## Executive assessment

Rough Cut 03 establishes that Episode 0000's production language transfers successfully to Episode 0001. The cut now inhabits the same dark Architectural Studio world, moves among related Whiteboard, Workspace and Focus surfaces, and develops its argument over time. It no longer reads primarily as nineteen isolated full-screen slides.

The transfer is not a scene-for-scene copy. Episode 0001 remains an architectural essay: repository evidence, questions, partial projections, knowledge relationships and human judgement are the dominant material. The Companion appears in seven selected scenes to introduce, bridge, question or reflect; twelve scenes remain content-led.

This is appropriately a rough cut, not a release candidate. Several long scenes still need continuous-playback pacing review, and five scenes identified below need targeted editorial refinement.

## Visual Identity

Episode 0001 now clearly belongs to the same series as Episode 0000. The environmental grid, restrained palette, raised working surfaces, persistent Articulate identity, typography and Companion lighting provide recognition across cuts. S001 establishes the Studio immediately; S019 closes within the same world instead of becoming a generic end slide.

Series coherence comes from reusable environments and behaviours rather than copied Episode 0000 diagrams. Episode 0001's radial projections, evidence synthesis, reasoning order and unresolved question remain distinct.

## PowerPoint Effect

The dominant Rough Cut 02 problem is substantially reduced. S001, S002, S004, S008, S009, S011, S014, S016, S018 and S019 now read as spaces or working surfaces rather than slides. Progressive reveals also prevent completed grids from arriving before the narration.

Residual slide-like tendencies remain in S006's seven-source inventory, S010's consequence accumulation and S017's outcome grouping. Their content is now timed and environmentally integrated, but the final states still resolve toward collections of labelled objects. These are refinement targets, not evidence that the baseline transfer failed.

## Companion

The increased presence works best in:

- S001, where the Companion establishes the programme and gives the opening a human centre;
- S004, where presence frames the architectural questions without answering them;
- S008, where the Companion bridges explanation into tool and repository evidence;
- S012–S014, where S012 and S013 create a human continuity bridge before S014 hands attention to the unresolved question;
- S017, where the Companion appropriately represents AI as a thoughtful collaborator alongside human responsibility;
- S019, where the return to the Studio gives the episode a genuine conclusion.

The Companion is unnecessary in the twelve content-led scenes. In particular, adding it to S005, S009, S010, S011, S015 or S016 would compete with diagrams that already carry the argument. RC03's motion is deliberately limited to idle, blink and subtle head/posture changes. There is no lip-sync because the Episode 0000 baseline did not establish it as an adopted production requirement.

## Environment

The episode now feels located within a coherent Articulate visual world. Adjacent scenes share palette, surface lighting, spatial margins and identity even when moving between Studio, Workspace, Whiteboard and Focus. Environment changes correspond to editorial function: Workspace for source evidence, Whiteboard for construction, Focus for concentrated relationships, and Studio for presence and reflection.

The strongest hand-offs are S001→S002→S003, S008→S009, S012→S013→S014 and S018→S019. They are continuous through shared visual anchors and surface language rather than decorative transition effects.

## Temporal Storytelling

All nineteen scenes now have authored progression, expressed through 53 editorial shots and their flattened deterministic timeline events. Information generally arrives when it becomes relevant and final states retain a readable hold.

Specific improvements include:

- S003 resolves in the narrated order “Not the diagrams” → “Not the documentation” → “The architecture,” with the final phrase emphasised;
- S004 reveals questions in spoken order;
- S005–S008 develop their concepts sequentially and S008 includes Confluence;
- S009 preserves progressive construction and makes “architecture in people's heads” literal;
- S010 accumulates consequences rather than opening on a complete grid;
- S014 introduces the question and supporting journal/repository evidence in stages;
- S016 uses a restrained balance metaphor for less searching and more problem-solving;
- S018 retains “Looking Ahead” as its editorial frame.

Continuous narration playback is still required to judge whether individual reveal offsets breathe naturally, especially in S007, S010, S014 and S015. Contact sheets prove order and state integrity, not perceived rhythm.

## Diagrams

S009 and S011 are the strongest evidence that the architectural material remains the star. S009 preserves the partial-projection model with directional construction. S011 restores the missing evidence relationships; the shared renderer regression is covered by a test. Directional connectors receive arrowheads and appear only when their source and destination are visible.

S015 includes the narrated architecture reasoning sequence and explicitly represents versioning, confidence and evidence. S016 replaces static comparison cards with a clearer shift-of-effort metaphor. S005's current-state → trade-offs/risk → future-state flow is legible and narration ordered.

Some final-state connector density remains in S015, and S006 still communicates fragmentation more as an inventory than as a genuinely incomplete composite. These require editorial refinement rather than a new diagram system.

## Remaining Weakest Scenes

The five scenes requiring the most editorial attention before a release candidate are:

1. **S006 — Scattered knowledge.** The corrected heading and progressive accumulation are clear, but the final arrangement still resembles an inventory. A more visibly partial or fractured composite would better express that no source is the whole truth.
2. **S007 — A living system.** The progression is valid, but the path metaphor does not yet make the rate of change versus the lag in understanding feel as distinct as the narration.
3. **S010 — The cost of fragmentation.** Temporal accumulation fixes the static-grid problem, yet the 45-second scene can still become visually dense. Review whether one consequence should dominate before the complete causal picture.
4. **S014 — The central question.** Repository/journal evidence prevents an isolated title-card hold, but the 37-second duration needs close playback review. A stronger evidence-to-question shift may be warranted.
5. **S015 — Principles before features.** The correct concepts are present and ordered, but the completed diagram has the highest connector density in the cut and may need routing or grouping refinement.
