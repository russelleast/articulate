# Episode 0001 Production Baseline Delta

Comparison: Episode 0001 Rough Cut 02 versus the published Episode 0000 production baseline  
Status: Completed before implementation; updated with the Rough Cut 03 decision record  
Decision purpose: identify reusable production capabilities before preparing the next Episode 0001 cut

## Reference authority

This comparison uses:

- Episode 0000 `final-cut-candidate-v2` as the latest production state in the commit identified as published;
- Episode 0000 `final-v1` and Production Quality Baseline v1 reviews as the approval evidence for the visual language;
- Episode 0001 Rough Cut 02 as the current Episode 0001 implementation;
- the available final-state and temporal contact sheets, render manifests, timing reports, asset manifests and production documentation for both episodes;
- Episode 0001 Rough Cut 01 and Rough Cut 02 editorial reviews as continuing requirements.

The Episode 0000 repository language is slightly inconsistent: `final-v1` is described as the approved complete render, while `final-cut-candidate-v2` is the latest state and the containing commit is labelled published even though its review retains a human-promotion gate. This delta therefore treats the v2 editorial and renderer changes as the current production capability baseline, but does not make facial performance or lip-sync mandatory for Episode 0001 without an explicit promotion decision.

## Executive conclusion

Episode 0001 does not need a new editorial structure. Its approved narration, narration-derived timing, nineteen scenes, architectural diagrams and progressive timeline work should be retained.

The meaningful production gap is this:

> Rough Cut 02 renders nineteen individually competent compositions. Episode 0000 renders one coherent architectural programme that moves between four related environments.

Episode 0001 already has more diagram types than Episode 0000. Adding more types would not solve the problem. The upgrade should instead apply Episode 0000's reusable production language:

1. a shared dark Architectural Studio environment around the working content;
2. a small family of purpose-led surfaces: Studio, Whiteboard, Workspace and Focus Canvas;
3. persistent series identity and spatial continuity across cuts;
4. narration-synchronised progression in every scene that needs it;
5. integrated, restrained Companion presence at the existing editorial bridges;
6. transitions expressed as relationships between ideas, not as isolated slide changes;
7. complete temporal review evidence, not only scene-final contact sheets.

The diagrams, repository evidence, questions and uncertainty in Episode 0001 remain the primary visual material. This initial assessment proposed retaining the Companion only in S001, S013 and S019. The subsequent Rough Cut 03 direction explicitly superseded that constraint and asked for broader continuity where the Companion adds presence without displacing architectural content. RC03 therefore uses restrained, motion-only Companion presence in seven scenes: S001, S004, S008, S012, S013, S017 and S019.

## Production-language delta

| Area | Episode 0001 Rough Cut 02 | Episode 0000 production baseline | Upgrade decision |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Overall frame | Warm, nearly full-frame paper field repeated across most scenes | Dark gridded environment containing lit working surfaces | Adopt the shared environment and surface relationship; do not simply recolour the existing slides. |
| Sense of place | Scene-local compositions with little spatial memory | Architectural Studio, Whiteboard, Workspace and Focus Canvas feel like related areas of one programme | Group Episode 0001 scenes by editorial environment and preserve anchors across adjacent scenes. |
| Composition | Headline, support and evenly weighted cards recur frequently | Asymmetrical surfaces, controlled negative space and a clear dominant object | Recompose around the diagram, evidence or question; reduce default headline/support/card geometry. |
| Scene taxonomy | Many content-specific renderer kinds | Four reusable production compositions with episode-owned layout modes | Keep Episode 0001 semantics, but resolve them through the proven environment family where appropriate. |
| Visual continuity | Mostly full-screen replacement at each scene boundary | Shared grid, palette, footer identity, surface lighting and repeated spatial anchors | Adopt directly. Continuity should come from the system, not copied scene layouts. |
| Progressive construction | Six scenes use timelines; thirteen render complete on entry | Every scene has authored progression, including title, evidence, diagrams and closing | Extend timeline authorship based on narration beats. Do not add decorative events to satisfy a count. |
| Long-scene pacing | Five scenes exceed 35 seconds; four remain static in Rough Cut 02 | No scene exceeds 35 seconds and all scenes develop over time | Give long scenes at least three meaningful states, following the Visual Grammar heuristic. |
| Companion integration | Neutral Companion sits against a pale blue rectangular panel in three isolated compositions | Companion is integrated into the Studio with warm light, shadow, floor contact, negative space and a working surface | Adopt the environmental treatment and select additional continuity beats by editorial purpose, not a screen-time quota. |
| Companion motion | Static | Deterministic idle motion is proven; the latest manifest can also carry separate facial and lip-sync performance timelines | Adopt restrained idle presence. Treat facial performance and lip-sync as optional pending an explicit promotion decision. |
| Diagrams | Strong episode-specific content, often presented as white cards on paper | Fewer layouts, progressively constructed on a persistent Whiteboard or Focus Canvas | Preserve Episode 0001 diagram meaning and geometry; move it onto the appropriate production surface. |
| Repository evidence | S002 uses an authored repository window; most other evidence is diagrammatic | Digital Workspace provides authentic repository areas, surrounding context and progressive evidence reveals | Upgrade S002 and use Workspace selectively for S006, S008, S014 or S018 where source context strengthens the claim. |
| Connectors | Directional boundaries and arrowheads are already proven in S009 | Destination-synchronised connectors, clean boundary termination, curved fan-out ports and aligned arrowheads | Reuse the mature connector policy across Episode 0001. Do not create episode-specific routing fixes. |
| Typography | Legible, but repeated presentation hierarchy makes many scenes feel like slides | Shared hierarchy changes with the environment; labels are short and surfaces carry the argument | Retain the shared type system but reduce headings/support copy that do not orient or focus the viewer. |
| Colour and depth | Predominantly flat paper, light cards and pale blue emphasis | Dark environmental depth, warm Companion light, raised paper surfaces and restrained rust emphasis | Adopt the semantic palette and depth cues. Preserve colour meaning across related Episode 0001 scenes. |
| Opening | Conventional light title composition with Companion panel | Immediate entry into the Studio; title and supporting ideas arrive on the working surface | Rebuild S001 as an in-world Episode 0001 opening, preserving its approved narration and short duration. |
| Section changes | Metadata says `section`, but runtime resolves to cuts; most cuts replace the full frame | Cuts remain restrained, but environmental continuity and shared anchors make them feel intentional | Keep deterministic cuts for now; improve the outgoing and incoming compositions before adding transition effects. |
| Intro-to-argument hand-off | S001 title cuts to S002 repository, then S003 cards | Studio can hand the viewer to Whiteboard, Workspace or Focus without a new title slide | Carry a word, surface edge, repository path or focal object from S001 through S003. |
| Ending | Separate light closing composition with Companion panel and source path | Studio invitation leads to a Focus Canvas hand-off into Episode 0001 | Let S019 close in the Studio while the journal/repository path becomes the final focus; avoid a generic end card. |
| Review evidence | Scene-final contact sheet plus timeline-resolution report | Authored-state contact sheet and fixed-interval temporal contact sheet expose progression and pacing | Enable both review modes for the upgraded cut. |
| Manifests and provenance | Complete and deterministic | Complete and deterministic, with Companion performance provenance where used | Retain. Extend only for capabilities actually adopted. |
| Publication package | No episode-local thumbnail system yet; captions remain a documented release requirement | Deterministic thumbnail candidates and reduced-size comparison previews exist | Treat publication artwork and captions as release work after the production treatment is approved. |

## What Episode 0001 should retain

The following are strengths, not deficiencies:

- approved narration and the complete 550.365692-second recording;
- the nineteen gap-free narration-derived scene boundaries;
- canonical journal traceability;
- the architectural argument and uncertainty of the episode;
- the current-state/change, fragmentation, living-system, projection, evidence, augmentation and principles diagrams;
- the directionally correct S009 projection model;
- the progressive event timing already authored for S002, S003, S004, S008, S009 and S010;
- the Companion's restrained supporting role, while allowing RC03 to expand its placement where it improves continuity;
- deterministic SVG/PNG rendering, FFmpeg assembly, validation and provenance;
- Visual Grammar ownership of layout, motion treatments and connector policy.

## Reusable Episode 0000 capabilities

### Adopt directly

- Architectural Studio environment and integrated working surface;
- Architectural Whiteboard, Digital Workspace and Focus Canvas composition family;
- shared safe area, grid, palette, lighting, footer identity and surface chrome;
- deterministic Companion idle transform;
- narration-relative reveal, hide, emphasize and connect timelines;
- destination-synchronised directional connectors;
- timeline-state and temporal contact-sheet review;
- overflow validation, deterministic assembly and manifest/provenance reporting.

### Adapt to Episode 0001

- Studio opening and closing layouts, using Episode 0001's approved title, source path and editorial purpose;
- Workspace treatment, using Episode 0001's real source and evidence rather than Episode 0000 repository copy;
- Whiteboard layouts, preserving Episode 0001's diagrams rather than copying Episode 0000 paths or hubs;
- Focus Canvas treatment for Episode 0001's conclusions, contrasts and unresolved central question;
- environmental hand-offs between adjacent scenes, based on Episode 0001's argument;
- Companion performance intensity, because Episode 0001 is essay-like and more than twice as long.

### Do not import by default

- Episode 0000 scene order, scene copy, diagram content or capability hierarchy;
- a Companion-led structure or increased Companion screen-time target;
- lip-sync merely because the runtime can express it;
- extra scene cuts that disturb the approved narration-derived structure;
- decorative animation, camera drift, new transition families or a general scripting framework;
- a requirement that every scene use the same surface or the same number of events.

## Priority gaps

### P0 — Required for the next full cut

1. Resolve Episode 0001 kinds through the shared Studio, Whiteboard, Workspace and Focus environment family.
2. Recompose the nineteen scenes so diagrams and evidence dominate instead of repeated headline/support/card scaffolding.
3. Author progression for the thirteen currently static scenes, prioritising S005, S007, S014, S015, S011, S012 and S017.
4. Preserve and refine the six existing timelines rather than replacing them.
5. Integrate the Companion into selected Studio scenes with restrained deterministic idle motion and no implied lip-sync.
6. Produce timeline-state and temporal contact sheets for the complete cut.

### P1 — Required for production-candidate review

1. Strengthen S001→S002→S003, S008→S009, S012→S013→S014 and S018→S019 as visual hand-offs.
2. Ground S006, S008, S014 and S018 in current repository or journal evidence where it improves the argument.
3. Review every reveal against continuous narration playback and recorded pauses.
4. Confirm reduced-size text and connector readability after video encoding.
5. Record whether the Episode 0000 facial/lip-sync candidate is promoted before deciding whether Episode 0001 may use it.

### P2 — Publication completion

1. Produce reviewed captions/transcript from the approved recording.
2. Create Episode 0001 publication thumbnail candidates using the established publication workflow.
3. Complete accessibility, publication metadata and final human approval gates.

## Acceptance criteria for the upgrade

The next Episode 0001 candidate should meet all of the following:

- approved narration, checksum, duration, scene count, scene order and gap-free coverage remain unchanged;
- all nineteen scenes either contain narration-relevant authored progression or record a deliberate editorial reason for stillness;
- every scene over 35 seconds contains at least three meaningful visual states unless deliberately justified;
- diagrams, repository evidence and questions remain the dominant teaching surfaces;
- Companion placement is justified scene by scene and remains subordinate to diagrams, evidence and questions;
- Companion treatment includes environmental integration and restrained idle presence without implying authorship;
- no consecutive run feels like repeated full-screen paper slides with the same headline/support/card hierarchy;
- connector direction, boundary termination and reveal timing follow the mature Visual Grammar policy;
- opening, major hand-offs and closing feel part of one series environment;
- scene-final, authored-state and temporal review artefacts are generated;
- deterministic rerendering, manifests, provenance, overflow validation and media checks continue to pass;
- human continuous playback confirms reveal timing, readability, pacing and the final trailing hold.

## Architectural decision

Implementation revealed one genuinely reusable capability beyond the original delta: a declarative shot hierarchy for grouping scene-relative timeline events around editorial beats. The decision and ownership boundary are recorded in [ADR 0005](../../../architecture/decisions/0005-declarative-shot-hierarchy.md). Shots do not introduce new narration boundaries, renderer scripts or composition-specific animation.

## Rough Cut 03 implementation record

RC03 applies this baseline without changing the approved recording, its checksum, the 550.365692-second duration, the nineteen scene boundaries or their order. Every scene now has authored temporal progression. The shared environment family is used as a production language, while Episode 0001's projections, evidence synthesis, reasoning sequence, uncertainty and human judgement remain the principal material.

The initial three-scene Companion recommendation above was a baseline-delta judgement, not an architectural constraint. The continued brief superseded it. RC03 uses seven Companion scenes and twelve content-led scenes; performance is deterministic idle/facial presence only, with no generated speech or lip-sync.
