## Segment N001

- Pause notes: pause after the opening claim
- Retake notes: keep delivery quiet and direct

Narration:

> Architecture is not difficult only because systems are complex. It is difficult because architectural knowledge is fragmented, constantly changing and never complete. This opening episode explains why Articulate exists.

## Segment N002

- Corresponding scene: S002
- Source reference: Introduction
- On-screen text: Understanding the architecture itself
- Pause notes: brief pause after "the architecture"

Narration:

> After decades building software and more than a decade working as an architect, Russell's observation is that modern technology is rarely the only constraint. Platforms, frameworks, infrastructure tools and AI are all remarkably capable. Yet one problem remains consistent: understanding the architecture itself.

## Segment N003

- Corresponding scene: S003
- Source reference: Introduction
- On-screen text: What changed? Can we trust the documentation?

Narration:

> Architects spend a surprising amount of time answering simple questions. What does the current architecture look like? Why was it designed this way? Which decisions still hold? What happens if we change this component? Can the documentation be trusted?

## Segment N004

- Corresponding scene: S004
- Source reference: Architecture Is About Change
- On-screen text: Architecture enables change

Narration:

> Architecture exists to enable change. Architects are not employed simply to draw diagrams. They help organisations make good decisions about change: introducing capabilities, reducing risk, balancing trade-offs and understanding how today's architecture can safely evolve into tomorrow's.

## Segment N005

- Corresponding scene: S005
- Source reference: The Reality of Modern Architecture
- On-screen text: Part of the truth

Narration:

> The difficulty is that architectural knowledge is rarely found in one place. It is scattered across source code, infrastructure, ADRs, diagrams, slide decks, monitoring platforms and the memories of people. Each source contains part of the truth. None contains the whole picture.

## Segment N006

- Corresponding scene: S006
- Source reference: The Limits of Today's Tools
- On-screen text: One projection of reality

Narration:

> The industry already has excellent tools. The problem is not that those tools are poor. The problem is that each one represents a projection of reality. A diagram is a view. An ADR records a decision. Source code records implementation. The architect is left connecting the pieces together.

## Segment N007

- Corresponding scene: S007
- Source reference: The Cost of Fragmented Knowledge
- On-screen text: More searching, less reasoning

Narration:

> Fragmented knowledge increases uncertainty. Current-state discovery takes longer. Decisions are forgotten and repeated. Impact analysis becomes manual investigation. Documentation loses relevance. Architects spend more time searching for information and less time reasoning about solutions.

## Segment N008

- Corresponding scene: S008
- Source reference: Why Now?
- On-screen text: Augment, not replace

Narration:

> The reason this is worth revisiting now is not that AI can generate code. It is that large language models can reason across large collections of unstructured information. They can identify patterns, summarise documents, highlight contradictions, ask follow-up questions and work with incomplete evidence. None of that replaces architectural thinking. It can augment it.

## Segment N009

- Corresponding scene: S009
- Source reference: The Vision
- On-screen text: Can architecture become a continuously evolving body of knowledge?

Narration:

> Articulate began as a side project and grew into a broader question. Can architecture become a continuously evolving body of knowledge instead of a collection of disconnected documents? That question sits at the heart of the project.

## Segment N010

- Corresponding scene: S010
- Source reference: Principles Before Features
- On-screen text: Principles before features

Narration:

> The journal begins with principles before features. Before choosing services, databases, frameworks or AI models, it asks what it means to version architecture, model confidence, preserve evidence and keep humans in control.

## Segment N011

- Corresponding scene: S011
- Source reference: Success
- On-screen text: Less searching. Better conversations.

Narration:

> Articulate will not succeed simply by producing better diagrams or more documentation. It will succeed if it helps preserve architectural knowledge, reduce uncertainty and improve the conversations architects have about change.

## Segment N012

- Corresponding scene: S012
- Source reference: Looking Ahead
- On-screen text: An exploration into better architecture

Narration:

> This first episode intentionally avoids implementation. It establishes the motivation for the work. Future episodes will explore the principles, requirements, constraints and experiments that shape Articulate as it evolves.
